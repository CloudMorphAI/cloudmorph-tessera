# The Funding

## Current status: bootstrapped

CloudMorph AI has no external funding. No venture capital, no angel investors, no grants,
no accelerator money. The company is self-funded.

The founder's full-time employment at Telus covers living costs and frees up CloudMorph's
own revenue — once it exists — to go back into the product rather than into salaries. That
is the current model.

## Why no VC

This is a deliberate choice, not a reflection of what was available.

The early MCP ecosystem is moving fast and the right shape of the product is not yet clear.
Taking external funding at this stage would mean committing to a growth trajectory and
reporting obligations before the fundamentals are understood. That is a poor trade.

More practically: Tessera is an open-source project. OSS businesses take longer to monetize
than pure SaaS, and the typical VC timeline does not fit that arc well. An investor
optimizing for a 3-year outcome would push the project away from what makes it valuable —
building genuine trust in the community — toward what makes it fundable — metrics that look
good in a deck.

Controlled growth and community credibility are more important to this project than
accelerated growth.

## How the project is funded going forward

The plan is to reach self-sustaining revenue through Tessera Cloud — the hosted version of
the firewall — before the bootstrapped runway runs out. The revenue model is straightforward:

- The OSS version is free and always will be.
- Tessera Cloud charges for the managed service above a free tier.

There are no hidden mechanisms here. If the hosted product generates enough revenue to
sustain the team and fund continued OSS development, the model works. If it does not, we
will say so.

## What we will not do

We will not take funding that requires us to:

- Add artificial limitations to the OSS version to drive cloud upgrades.
- Move the core engine to a proprietary license.
- Prioritize enterprise sales over the developer community that makes OSS valuable.

If the funding picture changes materially — for example, if we take on external investment
to scale the cloud product — we will update this page and explain the reasoning.
